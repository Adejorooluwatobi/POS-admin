// Tenant Onboarding Flow — How a New Business Gets On Your Platform
01
Business Signs Up
Walmart Nigeria registers on your platform, picks a plan (Starter / Pro / Enterprise)
👑 Auto or you approve
02
Tenant Created
A Tenant record is created. They get a unique slug (e.g. "walmart-ng") and a Tenant Admin account.
🏢 System auto-creates
03
Register Branches
Tenant Admin logs into admin portal and creates their Store records (VI branch, Ikeja branch, etc.)
🏪 Tenant Admin does this
04
Add Staff & Products
Tenant Admin or Store Manager adds cashiers, products, and configures terminals per branch.
👤 Manager does this
05
Go Live
Cashiers PIN login at terminals. Sales start flowing. Data stays isolated under their TenantId.
💳 POS is live
06
You Monitor
You watch all tenants from your Super Admin dashboard. Metrics, revenue, subscription status.
👑 Platform view only
// Color Coding — What Changed vs Before
NEW Tables
Tenant, TenantSubscription — the two new tables that make everything multi-tenant.
UPDATED Tables
Store gets TenantId FK. Staff gets SystemRole enum. Both tables gain one column each.
UNCHANGED — Row-Level Tenant Isolation
All other entities inherit tenant isolation via Store.TenantId. No other table needs TenantId directly because everything is Store-scoped.
EF Core Query Filters
HasQueryFilter on Store → IsActive AND TenantId == currentTenantId. All child queries inherit the filter automatically.
